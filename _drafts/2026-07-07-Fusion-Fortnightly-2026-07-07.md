[Realta claims first direct energy conversion](https://realtafusion.com/fusion-first-realta-demos-direct-energy-conversion/). The system gathered "multiple amps of current at around 100 volts". This feels to me like Realta did this entirely to beat Helion to be the first, not because it was a critical demonstration. Direct energy conversion is not new in mirrors, having been [considered](https://inis.iaea.org/records/ae6cf-tbn64/files/6176387.pdf) for many decades and [first demonstrated](https://www.ralphmoir.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/fusion-science-and-technology.pdf) at least 45 years ago. As I stated last time this came up with Helion: basically every fusion experiment has had some sort of "direct energy" conversion from the input and fusion energy in the plasma to electricity. That's what most (all?) measurement systems do. The largest likely conversion of energy in a plasma to electricity is with the central solenoid in tokamaks when the plasma current is ramped down at [JET with a 7 MA plasma](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/abs/10.13182/FST89-A39714), which is about 50 MJ of stored inductive energy at probably about 1 MW or so.

[STEP Fusion: Overview of the Concept Design for the Spherical Tokamak for Energy Production and Lessons Learnt](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10894-026-00589-5) published in Journal of Fusion Energy. STEP still aims for a spherical tokamak (it's baked in the name) by 2040 that can produce at least 100 MWe net and be tritium fuel self-sufficient. The major update from SPP-1 to SPP-2 is due to center-column magnet lifetime and blanket feasibility. The major radius increased from 3.6 m to 4.275 m, raising the projected centre-column lifetime from 0.22 to 0.6 full-power years. This is a common trend as the design fidelity increases, important issues like neutron sheilding reveal themselves to be harder as design fidelity increases (more holes!). The breader blanket changed from liquid lithium to a solid lithium oxide.

Spring Valley Acquisition Corp. III filed a [Rule 425 communication](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/2074850/000110465926077133/tm2618668d2_425.htm) that put General Fusion’s LM26 compressional-heating results into the securities-law record while the de-SPAC solicitation was live. The filing does more than publicize a technical claim. It ties the claimed milestone to a pending shareholder vote, expected Nasdaq symbols, peer-review status, and a risk frame that expressly covers scientific validation, repeatability, financing, litigation, listing, and deal-completion risk. For investors, deal counsel, and expert-witness work, this is the cleanest in-window filed source linking a fusion performance claim to the active public-market transaction record.

Spring Valley also filed a [Rule 425 communication](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/2074850/000110465926077355/tm2618668d1_425.htm) covering General Fusion’s framework agreement with Renexia for potential commercial deployment in Italy. The legal value is not the publicity alone; it is that the filed communication places into the SEC record a named strategic counterparty, a milestone-based deployment structure, and contemplated phases that run from site evaluation to offtake, permitting, funding, construction, and commissioning, with further definitive agreements anticipated. That makes it material to diligence on commercialization pathway, counterparty seriousness, project-development sequencing, and the extent to which transaction communications are staking out future commercial representations.

Spring Valley’s [Form 8-K](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/2074850/000110465926078975/tm2617090d4_8k.htm) furnished an updated investor presentation for the General Fusion transaction and expressly stated that the new deck supersedes the earlier version previously furnished. That matters because it refreshes the solicitation record after the registration statement became effective and proxy materials were mailed, giving investors and litigators a later, formal snapshot of the transaction narrative, milestones, and financing posture being used with shareholders and other audiences. The filing is also legally useful because it clarifies the presentation’s status under Item 7.01 as furnished rather than filed for Section 18 purposes unless expressly incorporated elsewhere.
