[Commonwealth Fusion Systems posted a SPARC project update](https://blog.cfs.energy/cfs-prepares-sparcs-fusion-support-systems-for-dress-rehearsal/). CFS said its cryogenic plant is operating and circulating cryogenic fluid through magnet-cooling subsystems, and that the upcoming "dry dress rehearsal" will run the plant as if powering a fusion pulse while tokamak assembly continues in parallel. Standard plant comissioning stuff, bringing systems online for operation.

[China requests public comment on the draft “Safety Analysis Work Guideline for Deuterium-Tritium Fusion Experimental Devices"](https://www.mee.gov.cn/xxgk2018/xxgk/xxgk06/202607/t20260716_1161973.html). 

[General Fusion completes deSPAC with Spring Valley Acquisition Corp. III](https://generalfusion.com/post/general-fusion-completes-business-combination-with-spring-valley-acquisition-corp-iii/). General Fusion began trading on Nasdaq on July 13 under [GFUZ](https://www.nasdaq.com/market-activity/stocks/gfuz). It announced it entered the public market with about $150 million in cash, indicating about 70% of the initial SPAC investors redeemed their shares. This is not unusual for GFUZ's deSPACing peers, but many deSPACs fade away.

[Realta Fusion announced moving its headquarters to the former Oscar Mayer plant in Madison](https://wedc.org/realta-fusion-to-relocate-corporate-headquarters-to-former-oscar-mayer-plant-in-madison/). The city and state have committed up to $55M to support this move, including tax exemptions/credits. Next week CFS will announce its relocation to the Dunkin Donuts bakery and Proxima to the Rischart Bavarian bakery. Who's next in their move to a facility that made food in the shape of their plasma?

[F4E publishes the first Global Fusion Energy Trends report](https://fusionforenergy.europa.eu/wp-content/uploads/2026/07/F4E_Observatory-Global_Fusion_Energy_Trends_1st_Edition.pdf). This is a really nice compelation of the state of international (China, EU, Japan, UK, and US) fusion development. There are really useful figures putting in construction machines in context of what has already been acieved and timelines of planned facilities. It also shows data on things like publications and patents (China is patenting a lot but only domestically!). Finally, there's a comparison of the regulatory timelines in the major regions. We're seeing different strategies unfold in each region, which one will be the most successful to develop fusion?

[New facility in Denmark to test remote handling robotics for fusion](https://euro-fusion.org/eurofusion-news/new-facility-in-denmark-will-let-engineers-test-robots-in-handling-key-fusion-components/). 

[New research consortium tests materials for fusion power plants](https://www.ipp.mpg.de/start-research-consortium-fuma?c=5594164). It combines expertise and experimental capability in high heat flux, neutron irradiation, and material characterization.

[Kyoto Fusioneering announced a strategic partnership with NGK to jointly develop and commercialize FLiBe](https://kyotofusioneering.com/en/news/2026/07/16/4085). NKG, a leading producer of berylium copper, helps a lot on the berylium handling side of things.

[Type One Energy hosted an open house near its planned Tennessee facility](https://typeoneenergy.com/blog/a-community-first-approach-to-fusion-energy/). More than 150 people attended.
