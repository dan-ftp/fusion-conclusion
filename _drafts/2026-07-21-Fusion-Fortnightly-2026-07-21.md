[Commonwealth Fusion Systems is preparing SPARC’s fusion support systems for dress rehearsal](https://blog.cfs.energy/cfs-prepares-sparcs-fusion-support-systems-for-dress-rehearsal/). CFS said its cryogenic plant is operating and circulating cryogenic fluid through magnet-cooling subsystems, and that the upcoming rehearsal will run the plant as if powering a fusion pulse while tokamak assembly continues in parallel. Standard plant comissioning stuff, bringing systems online for operation.

[China requests public comment on the draft “Safety Analysis Work Guideline for Deuterium-Tritium Fusion Experimental Devices"](https://www.mee.gov.cn/xxgk2018/xxgk/xxgk06/202607/t20260716_1161973.html). 

[UKAEA did updates its Procurement Pipeline](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/ukaea-procurement-pipeline).

[General Fusion completes deSPAC with Spring Valley Acquisition Corp. III](https://generalfusion.com/post/general-fusion-completes-business-combination-with-spring-valley-acquisition-corp-iii/). General Fusion began trading on Nasdaq on July 13 under [GFUZ](https://www.nasdaq.com/market-activity/stocks/gfuz). It announced it entered the public market with about $150 million in cash. The materially new development in this window is that the deal actually closed, making General Fusion the first publicly listed pure-play fusion company rather than merely a proposed transaction. That matters because it opens a new capital path for fusion developers and gives investors a public benchmark for the sector, although the company still has no commercial revenue and says key technical milestones remain ahead.

- [Global fusion energy annual investments hit record of nearly $4.5 billion](https://www.reuters.com/business/energy/global-fusion-energy-annual-investments-hit-record-nearly-45-billion-2026-07-13/) Reuters reported on July 13 that annual private fusion investment reached a record $4.48 billion across 56 companies, up 69% from a year earlier, with cumulative private investment above $14.2 billion since 2021. The new fact in this window is the record annual funding figure itself, published through the Fusion Industry Association’s latest survey at a moment when General Fusion has just listed and TAE is still pursuing a public-market route. That matters for investors, suppliers, and policymakers because it points to deeper capital availability and a broader commercialization push tied to future power demand from data centers and AI, although the survey also says China’s private-sector picture is still incomplete.

- [Gov. Evers, WEDC Announce Realta Fusion to Relocate Corporate Headquarters to Former Oscar Mayer Plant in Madison, Boosting State’s Carbon-Free Electricity Efforts](https://wedc.org/realta-fusion-to-relocate-corporate-headquarters-to-former-oscar-mayer-plant-in-madison/) Wisconsin officials announced on July 15 that Realta Fusion will place its headquarters and fusion R&D facility at the former Oscar Mayer site in Madison, where it expects to start building its Hammir prototype machine before year-end. What is genuinely new is that Realta moved from a site search to a named location with explicit state backing and linkage to Wisconsin’s wider fusion-commercialization push. That matters because site readiness and local political support are still scarce in fusion, and this gives utilities, suppliers, and regional policymakers a more concrete U.S. industrial cluster to track.
