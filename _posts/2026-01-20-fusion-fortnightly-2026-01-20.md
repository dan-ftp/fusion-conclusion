<p><a href="https://www.reuters.com/business/energy/meta-strikes-nuclear-power-agreements-with-three-companies-2026-01-09/">Meta signs up for 6.6 GW of fission power by 2035</a> in agreements with Vistra, TerraPower, and Oklo. This is about the same amount of power the city-state of <a href="https://www.ema.gov.sg/resources/singapore-energy-statistics/chapter3"> Singapore consumes</a>!</p>
<p><a href="https://world-nuclear-news.org/articles/terrestrial-energy-oklo-execute-DOE-agreements">Terrestrial Energy and Oklo</a> sign <a href="https://acqnotes.com/acqnote/careerfields/other-transaction-authority-ota">Other Transaction Authority</a> (OTA) with the U.S. Department of Energy (DOE). The OTAs let these companies rapidly construct and operate experimental reactors on federal sites, with companies bearing costs but gaining agile regulatory approval, not having to go through lengthy Nuclear Regulatory Commission approval processes.</p>
