[ITER vacuum vessel ruled by regulators to not be governed by same regulations as fission pressure vessel](https://www.iter.org/actualites-iter/key-ruling-iter-vessel-strengthens-fit-purpose-fusion-regulatory-approach). This highlights one of the risks of going the route the French did with ITER and regulating fusion under a fission framework: there are more differences than similarities and it is too easy to apply the fission rules wrongly to fusion. From an engineering standpoint the ITER vacuum vessel is indeed a pressure vessel. But, as the article states the electromagnetic loads from disruptions are more often than not the ones that dominate the engineering design. Simply, if it can handle the electromagnetic loads it can handle the external atmospheric pressure loads. This change is simply recognizing that fact and applying the appropriate safety/testing/etc. criteria to the vacuum vessel.
