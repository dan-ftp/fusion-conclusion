*No fluff, all facts.*

[Type One Energy, Tokamak Energy, and AECOM form the UK Infinity Fusion Consortium]([https://www.world-nuclear-news.org/articles/british-us-consortium-aims-to-build-uk-fusion-plant](https://aecom.com/press-releases/type-one-energy-tokamak-energy-and-aecom-form-the-uk-infinity-fusion-consortium-to-accelerate-development-of-a-commercial-fusion-power-plant-in-the-united-kingdom/)). This is a distinct effort from the [UK's STEP](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spherical_Tokamak_for_Energy_Production) fusion power plant prototype. [Type One Energy](https://typeoneenergy.com/) is an American stellarator company. [Tokamak Energy](https://tokamakenergy.com/) is a British company that was a spherical tokamak company but has recently pivoted to being a [superconducting magnet manufacturer and supplier](https://tokamakenergy.com/our-fusion-energy-and-hts-technology/te-magnetics/). [AECOM](https://aecom.com/) is an American multinational infrastructure consulting firm that knows how to build big stuff. I find it interesting that Tokamak Energy is the magnet supplier for Type One Energy in this consortium: just over a year ago it was [announced](https://cfs.energy/news-and-media/commonwealth-fusion-systems-and-type-one-energy-sign-fusion-magnet-agreement/) that "...Type One Energy [gets] an exclusive license to use CFS’ groundbreaking high-temperature superconducting (HTS) cable technology in the development of its own proprietary stellarator fusion magnets. The agreement also gives Type One Energy the benefit of the experience CFS has in manufacturing the world’s most powerful HTS fusion magnets."

[South Korea launches its fifth basic fusion-energy plan](https://www.yna.co.kr/view/AKR20260507101400017), with a 56-member committee and a stated goal of demonstrating fusion power generation in the 2030s. 

[DOE and Max Planck lock in a new 10-year W7-X partnership](https://www.pppl.gov/news/2026/transatlantic-fusion-energy-research-just-got-easier). 

[Zap Energy expans from fusion to fission and fission-fusion]([https://techcrunch.com/2026/04/29/fusion-power-startup-zap-energy-pulls-a-partial-pivot-adding-nuclear-fission-to-the-mix/](https://d2h9ly8owvvy4f.cloudfront.net/uploads/Zap-Energy-Integrated-Approach-Fission-Fusion.pdf)). It had to happen eventually. Increasing the capacity of a fusion device from a good producer of neutrons to an economic source of energy is really hard and may be impossible for many of the fusion reactor concepts. Concepts have been around for decades on [fusion-fission hybrids](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nuclear_fusion%E2%80%93fission_hybrid) where a fusion neutron source that's not good enough on its own to make economic fusion energy is combined with a fission fuel that's not good enough on its own to make a self-sustaining fission reactor. 

[ITER says its turnaround has put the project back on budget and on schedule](https://www.nucnet.org/news/iter-european-fusion-project-now-on-budget-and-on-schedule-after-turnaround-5-5-2026). On May 8, NucNet reported ITER management saying the project had entered a critical assembly phase and was now on budget and on schedule after its turnaround effort. What is new is the claimed restoration of cost and schedule discipline as assembly intensifies, a notable contrast with the delay-reset narrative of the past two years. That matters because ITER remains the largest source of reactor-scale engineering lessons for public labs and private suppliers, though this is management reporting rather than an outside audit.

[Chinese researchers report a one-minute high-performance tokamak regime with lower heat loads](https://phys.org/news/2026-04-tokamak-regime-sustains-stable-fusion.html). A Phys.org report published on April 29 highlighted a new Physical Review Letters result from China’s EAST team: a minute-long operating regime that combined partial divertor detachment, ELM-free H-mode, and high pedestal performance in a metal-wall tokamak. What is new is the simultaneous handling of two of fusion’s hardest engineering problems — heat exhaust and confinement quality — in a single long-pulse regime. That matters because reactor economics and uptime depend on surviving heat loads without wrecking plasma performance, although this remains a physics milestone rather than a plant-scale commercial proof.

[Startorus Fusion closes a 500 million yuan A+ round and crosses unicorn status](https://finance.eastmoney.com/a/202605073730027724.html). Chinese financial media reported on May 7 that Shanghai-based Startorus Fusion had raised 500 million yuan in an A+ round, taking cumulative funding above 2 billion yuan and valuation above $1 billion. The new development is not just another early-stage raise: the money is earmarked for Jiading base construction, NTST installation and operation, reactor-design iteration, fusion-grade high-temperature-superconductor magnet production, and AI plasma-control engineering, only months after a 1 billion yuan Series A. That matters because it suggests China’s private fusion market is moving from research teams toward capitalized engineering programs, though the report comes through Chinese media rather than a global wire and funding alone does not prove execution.
