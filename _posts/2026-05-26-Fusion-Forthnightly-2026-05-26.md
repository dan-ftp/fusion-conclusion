*No fluff, all facts.*

Date range: 2026-05-12 to 2026-05-25 (inclusive).

[General Fusion appointed Thomas Boehlert to its board](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/2074850/000110465926065173/tm2615290d1_425.htm). [Thomas](https://www.linkedin.com/in/thomasmboehlert/details/experience/) has a a long career as a CFO in various energy and more recently mineral companies. He will be the chair of the nominating and governance committees at General Fusion. More shoring up of their Board and leadership as General Fusion works to go public.

[TAE and UKAEA announced that TAE Beam UK had been formally established and was fully funded](https://tae.com/tae-technologies-ukaea-advance-development-of-core-fusion-technology/). They're working together, using their experience building and operating neutral beams to make them smaller and cheaper for fusion energy. To me, this is one of the most worthwhile things TAE is doing to advance fusion energy. Neutral beams have been the workhorse of heating for many fusion experiments but, like much of fusion, have been too big and expensive to be practical for economic fusion applications. Case in point: [F4E and ALSYMEX celebrate the completion of the MITICA beam source](https://fusionforenergy.europa.eu/news/mitica-beam-source-completion-alsymex/).

[Commonwealth Fusion Systems announced that the second half of SPARC’s vacuum vessel had arrived and that fit-out work had begun](https://blog.cfs.energy/cfs-begins-preparing-a-home-for-our-100-million-degree-fusion-plasma/). Brandon gives a nice video tour at the end of the article.

[Thea Energy successfully operated its first full-size, full-current, and full-field planar shaping coil](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/05/13/3293984/0/en/thea-energy-announces-successful-operation-of-full-scale-planar-shaping-coils.html). The company said the magnet reached the fields (6 T) and currents required for its upcoming Eos stellarator. 

[Proxima Fusion created of an Industrial Development Board](https://www.proximafusion.com/press-news/proxima-fusion-convenes-industrial-development-board-to-scale-europes-fusion-future). The new body brings in senior industrial and energy figures including former EDF chief [Luc Rémont](https://www.linkedin.com/in/luc-r%C3%A9mont/), former Bosch CTO [Michael Bolle](https://www.linkedin.com/in/michael-bolle-45764829/), former VP of Breakthrough Energy Ventures, Eurpoe [Ann Mettler](https://www.linkedin.com/in/annmettler/), and E.ON chair [Erich Clementi](https://www.linkedin.com/in/erich-clementi/). Promixa continues to demonstrate its skill in coallition building.

[Astral Systems, together with Promation and McMaster University, was awarded CMIE funding through the Scale-67 project](https://nuclear.mcmaster.ca/2026/05/mcmaster-joins-promation-and-astral-systems-for-cmie-funded-medical-isotope-project/). McMaster said the program will receive $500k to develop a fusion reaction-based process for [Copper-67](https://www.sciencedirect.com/topics/pharmacology-toxicology-and-pharmaceutical-science/copper-67), which is used for diagnostic imaging and cancer treatment.

The recent news of the [DOE announcing more than $94 million of Gen III+ SMR pathway awards to eight companies](https://www.energy.gov/articles/energy-department-awards-94-million-american-companies-help-expedite-deployments-small), [DOE’s Office of Critical Minerals and Energy Innovation announcing $45.7 million for 19 critical-material projects](https://www.energy.gov/cmei/articles/does-office-critical-minerals-and-energy-innovation-announces-over-45-million-support), and the [Department of Commerce Announcing letters of intent with 9 companies for $2B to accelerate U.S. leadership in quantum computing](https://www.nist.gov/news-events/news/2026/05/department-commerce-announces-letters-intent-9-companies-2-billion) as well as the [$800M in federal cost-shared funding for fission announced at the end of last year](https://www.energy.gov/articles/energy-department-selects-tva-and-holtec-advance-deployment-us-small-modular-reactors) has me wondering what is in store for fusion...

[Thea Energy adds Paul Wilkins as vice president of policy](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/thea_paul-wilkins-just-joined-thea-energy-as-vice-activity-7463272400890150912-7gg5). [Paul](https://www.linkedin.com/in/paul-wilkins-726232116/) was Chief of Staff to a U.S. senator as well as VP of policy at Bloom Energy and Electric Hydrogen.

[Type One Energy brings former ORNL fusion section head Larry Baylor in as engineering fellow](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/type-one-energy_our-world-class-team-of-fusion-scientists-activity-7463264346547097600-B_x7). [Larry](https://www.linkedin.com/in/larry-baylor-0643008/) has expertise across a variety of fusion energy systems but he's most well known in the community as the go-to expert for systems to fuel the plasma with frozen pellets. And aparently, from his LinkedIn profile, he's good at threading golfballs through a flock of Canada geese.

[Pacific Fusion moves co-founder Leland Ellison into the vice president of technology role](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/pacificfusion_pacific-fusion-co-founder-leland-ellison-activity-7463303493404213248-28vH). [Leland](https://www.linkedin.com/in/clelandellison/) is a career computational physicist.

[General Fusion is exploring listing on the Toronto Stock Exchange in addition to the Nasdaq](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/2074850/000110465926065854/tm2615290d2_425.htm). 

[General Fusion files Amendment No. 3 to the SEC Form F-4](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/2074850/000110465926060481/0001104659-26-060481-index.htm), [a 10-Q](https://www.sec.gov/ix?doc=/Archives/edgar/data/2074850/000110465926061398/none-20260331x10q.htm), and [a K-8](https://www.sec.gov/ix?doc=/Archives/edgar/data/2074850/000110465926063244/tm2614919d1_8k.htm). They have some new info on legal and captial structure but nothing on how they are going to adiabatically compress their plasmas.

[American Fusion filed its Form 10-Q for the quarter ended March 31, 2026](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/96664/000107997326000711/rnwf_10q-033126.htm). While nothing of consequence as progress to fusion energy, reading through these is a fascinating look into the world of penny stocks and companies that may not be what they seem to be. It disclosed less than $100k of cash at quarter-end and said substantial doubt exists about the company’s ability to continue as a going concern while management pursues a 2026 capital raise. The chairman, [Brent Nelson](https://www.linkedin.com/in/brent-nelson-20134397/), has a 60% controlling share. The consulting and independent director advisory agreements have a total compensation of $1.68 million in common stock. The share count is determined by market price: if the stock is lower one year later, the company must issue more shares so the recipients still receive the agreed value; if the stock rises, there is no clawback.

[First Light Fusion has some new filings related to their recent round](https://find-and-update.company-information.service.gov.uk/company/07555858/filing-history). [Adrian Gareth Jones](https://www.linkedin.com/in/adrian-gareth-jones/) and [Theodore Frederic Florent Marie Mollinger](https://www.linkedin.com/in/theodoremollinger/) are appointed as directors. The documents imply a post-money valuation of about £65M. 

[The National Institutes for Quantum Science and Technology has now contracted with six Japanese suppliers to execute an additional 20-unit ITER gyrotron package](https://www.qst.go.jp/site/press/20260520.html).

[China and Russia sign nuclear-related MoUs during Xi-Putin talks](https://www.world-nuclear-news.org/articles/china-and-russia-sign-nuclear-related-mous-during-xi-putin-summit). Although details are light, it almost certainly includes collaboration on China's new tokamak, BEST.

[Kyoto Fusioneering awarded contract by QST to develop tritium extraction and accountancy system prototypes for ITER test blanket system](https://kyotofusioneering.com/en/news/2026/05/15/3934). These are critical technologies that need to be developed to get the tritium out of where it's bred. 

[ITER Magnet Cold Test Facility operational](https://www.iter.org/sites/default/files/media/2026-05/2026-05-21_pr_magnet_facility.pdf). They used to say they didn't have the time to test the magnets after they are built but before they are assembled with the rest of the machine. Now that the schedule has slipped so much, they have the luxury of time to do so to "strengthen ITER’s risk mitigation and readiness."

[A*STAR and Commonwealth Fusion Systems sign collaborative research agreement](https://www.a-star.edu.sg/News/astarNews/news/press-releases/astar-commonwealth-fusion-supply-chain-singapore). The five-year agreement is to help develop fusion technologies for CFS' ARC and Singapore's building of a domestic industry to supply to the fusion industry. Reminder that Temasek, a Singapore sovereign wealth fund, is a large investor in CFS.
