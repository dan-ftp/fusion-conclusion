*No fluff, all facts.*

Date range: 2026-05-12 to 2026-05-25 (inclusive).

[General Fusion appointed Thomas Boehlert to its board](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/2074850/000110465926065173/tm2615290d1_425.htm). [Thomas](https://www.linkedin.com/in/thomasmboehlert/details/experience/) has a a long career as a CFO in various energy and more recently mineral companies. He will be the chair of the nominating and governance committees at General Fusion. More shoring up of their Board and leadership as General Fusion works to go public.

[American Fusion filed its Form 10-Q for the quarter ended March 31, 2026](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/96664/000107997326000711/rnwf_10q-033126.htm). While nothing of consequence as progress to fusion energy, reading through these is a fascinating look into the world of penny stocks and companies that may not be what they seem to be. It disclosed less than $100k of cash at quarter-end and said substantial doubt exists about the company’s ability to continue as a going concern while management pursues a 2026 capital raise. The chairman, [Brent Nelson](https://www.linkedin.com/in/brent-nelson-20134397/), has a 60% controlling share. The consulting and independent director advisory agreements have a total compensation of $1.68 million in common stock. The share count is determined by market price: if the stock is lower one year later, the company must issue more shares so the recipients still receive the agreed value; if the stock rises, there is no clawback.

[TAE and UKAEA announced that TAE Beam UK had been formally established and was fully funded](https://tae.com/tae-technologies-ukaea-advance-development-of-core-fusion-technology/). They're working together, using their experience building and operating neutral beams to make them smaller and cheaper for fusion energy. To me, this is one of the most worthwhile things TAE is doing to advance fusion energy. Neutral beams have been the workhorse of heating for many fusion experiments but, like much of fusion, have been too big and expensive to be practical for economic fusion applications.  

[Commonwealth Fusion Systems announced that the second half of SPARC’s vacuum vessel had arrived and that fit-out work had begun](https://blog.cfs.energy/cfs-begins-preparing-a-home-for-our-100-million-degree-fusion-plasma/). Brandon gives a nice video tour at the end of the article.

[Thea Energy successfully operated its first full-size, full-current, and full-field planar shaping coil](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/05/13/3293984/0/en/thea-energy-announces-successful-operation-of-full-scale-planar-shaping-coils.html). The company said the magnet reached the fields (6 T) and currents required for its upcoming Eos stellarator. 

[Proxima Fusion created of an Industrial Development Board](https://www.proximafusion.com/press-news/proxima-fusion-convenes-industrial-development-board-to-scale-europes-fusion-future). The new body brings in senior industrial and energy figures including former EDF chief [Luc Rémont](https://www.linkedin.com/in/luc-r%C3%A9mont/), former Bosch CTO [Michael Bolle](https://www.linkedin.com/in/michael-bolle-45764829/), former VP of Breakthrough Energy Ventures, Eurpoe [Ann Mettler](https://www.linkedin.com/in/annmettler/), and E.ON chair [Erich Clementi](https://www.linkedin.com/in/erich-clementi/). Promixa continues to demonstrate its skill in coallition building.

[Astral Systems, together with Promation and McMaster University, was awarded CMIE funding through the Scale-67 project](https://nuclear.mcmaster.ca/2026/05/mcmaster-joins-promation-and-astral-systems-for-cmie-funded-medical-isotope-project/). McMaster said the program will receive $500k to develop a fusion reaction-based process for [Copper-67](https://www.sciencedirect.com/topics/pharmacology-toxicology-and-pharmaceutical-science/copper-67), which is used for diagnostic imaging and cancer treatment.

The recent news of the [DOE announcing more than $94 million of Gen III+ SMR pathway awards to eight companies](https://www.energy.gov/articles/energy-department-awards-94-million-american-companies-help-expedite-deployments-small) and the [Department of Commerce Announcing letters of intent with 9 companies for $2B to accelerate U.S. leadership in quantum computing](https://www.nist.gov/news-events/news/2026/05/department-commerce-announces-letters-intent-9-companies-2-billion) as well as the [$800M in federal cost-shared funding for fission announced at the end of last year](https://www.energy.gov/articles/energy-department-selects-tva-and-holtec-advance-deployment-us-small-modular-reactors) has me wondering what is in store for fusion...

[DOE’s Office of Critical Minerals and Energy Innovation announced $45.7 million for 19 critical-material projects](https://www.energy.gov/cmei/articles/does-office-critical-minerals-and-energy-innovation-announces-over-45-million-support). The package includes pilot-scale work for magnesium and rare-earth processing, plus projects aimed at rare-earth metallic conversion, recycled-magnet separation, and pre-commercial U.S. mine-to-magnet pathways. This matters to fusion because magnet, cryogenic, actuator, and power-electronics supply chains all inherit critical-mineral exposure, so domestic rare-earth processing is a near-term hedge against the same upstream bottlenecks fusion developers may face.

General Fusion and Spring Valley put an amended joint registration statement on the SEC record through [F-4/A accession 0001104659-26-060481](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/2074850/000110465926060481/0001104659-26-060481-index.htm). For legal and diligence purposes, this is the operative securities-registration and proxy-prospectus refresh for the proposed business combination, not just marketing collateral. It matters because amended registration papers can reset how counsel, PIPE participants, redemption-sensitive holders, and expert witnesses read the disclosure package, transaction mechanics, and shareholder-solicitation record. Even without a stand-alone deal-term rewrite, a new F-4/A is itself a meaningful step in the live securities process around a would-be pure-play fusion listing.

[Spring Valley’s quarterly report, accession 0001104659-26-061398](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/2074850/000110465926061398/0001104659-26-061398-index.htm), materially updates the General Fusion transaction record in a place diligence teams actually use: the periodic-report file. The report restates the business-combination structure, flags the sponsor’s planned founder-share forfeiture, notes the intended transfer of founder shares to recent General Fusion SAFE investors, describes possible conversion of working-capital loans into warrants, and previews the post-closing registration-rights package. For investors, boards, and litigation teams, this is a compact source on dilution paths, closing conditions, sponsor economics, and securities-law representations embedded outside the headline merger announcement.

[Spring Valley’s current report on Amendment No. 1 to the business combination agreement, accession 0001104659-26-063244](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/2074850/000110465926063244/0001104659-26-063244-index.htm), is the clearest in-window legal step for the General Fusion deal. The filing says the amendment moves SPAC redemptions to immediately before the continuation into British Columbia, fixes the initial size of the post-closing equity incentive plan at 15% of post-close common shares, and swaps in new forms of the closing articles and plan of arrangement. Those are real transaction mechanics, not fluff, and they matter to redemption modeling, governance planning, and cross-border closing execution.

[This filed Rule 425 communication](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/0002074850/000110465926065173/tm2615290d1_425.htm) adds General Fusion governance directly into the securities-law record for the pending Spring Valley transaction by disclosing the appointment of Thomas Boehlert to the board and to chair the nominating and governance committee. Standing alone, a private-company board appointment might be ordinary. In an active merger-and-listing process, however, a filed 425 matters because it becomes part of the solicitation record and updates how public-market readiness, board composition, and governance oversight are framed for shareholders, counterparties, and later expert or litigation review.

[American Fusion’s NT 10-Q, accession 0001079973-26-000682](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/96664/000107997326000682/0001079973-26-000682-index.htm), is a small filing with outsized diligence value because it is the company’s first clear public admission that it could not complete its new reporting-company quarterly report on time. The notice attributes the delay to unfinished financial statements and MD&A and says the company expected to use the short grace period. For a newly reporting fusion issuer built from a reverse-merger history, that late notice matters to investors, auditors, lenders, and expert witnesses assessing reporting readiness, internal controls, and the practical strain of the post-transaction disclosure burden.

[American Fusion’s first-quarter Form 10-Q, accession 0001079973-26-000711](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/0000096664/000107997326000711/rnwf_10q-033126.htm), is the most important in-window public-company record outside the General Fusion SPAC file. It frames the Kepler Fusion transaction as a reverse recapitalization, discloses that a single preferred share carries 60% voting power, records the prepaid warrant financing facility and remaining availability, repeats substantial-doubt going-concern language, and folds legacy litigation and share-cancellation cleanup into the public record through subsequent-event disclosure. For business diligence, this filing is the core document on control, capitalization, liquidity, and merger-accounting posture at the first publicly reporting pure-play fusion vehicle.

[First Light Fusion’s Companies House filing history](https://find-and-update.company-information.service.gov.uk/company/07555858/filing-history) now shows a new SH01 statement of capital following a share allotment, with stated capital updated to GBP 1,283.173. That makes this more than routine housekeeping: it is the registry-level capitalization record for a major private fusion developer after a fresh equity issuance. For investors, acquirers, and expert witnesses, SH01 filings are useful because they convert financing and cap-table movement into a formal public record that can anchor dilution analysis, rights reviews, and reconstruction of who funded the company and on what share base.

[The same First Light Fusion filing-history page](https://find-and-update.company-information.service.gov.uk/company/07555858/filing-history) also records an MR04 satisfaction of charge in full for charge code 075558580001. That is a legally operative collateral event, not ordinary publicity, and it matters because it changes the public encumbrance record around a core fusion company. A charge satisfaction can affect lender priority analysis, negative-pledge drafting, freedom to refinance, and transaction diligence on whether key assets remain subject to historic security interests. For suppliers, lenders, and deal counsel, this is exactly the kind of quiet registry update that can materially change risk assessment.

- [Fusion energy poised for simpler U.S. review](https://www.axios.com/2026/05/22/fusion-energy-simpler-federal-review) brought the NRC’s proposed fusion framework into its final comment-period stretch, with comments due on May 27 and industry now openly discussing a final rule as soon as this fall. What is genuinely new in the window is not the February proposal itself but the approach of the first real U.S. regulatory endgame for fusion machines, which companies can start planning around. That matters because a lighter licensing path changes project timing, siting risk, and capital assumptions for developers, utilities, and large power buyers.

- [ジャイロトロンシステム20機をITER機構より受託](https://www.qst.go.jp/site/press/20260520.html) disclosed that QST has now contracted with six Japanese suppliers to execute an additional 20-unit ITER gyrotron package. What is new in the window is the May 20 public release of the supplier contracts signed in March and the move toward higher-performance units, with output per gyrotron rising from 1.0 MW to 1.2 MW. That matters because gyrotrons are a core plasma-heating bottleneck for ITER and later plants, so this is a concrete supply-chain and manufacturing-readiness signal rather than another generic fusion pledge.

- [F4E and ALSYMEX celebrate the completion of the MITICA beam source](https://fusionforenergy.europa.eu/news/mitica-beam-source-completion-alsymex/) marked completion of assembly and factory tests for the full-size prototype beam source for ITER’s neutral-beam heating system. What is genuinely new in the window is that Europe declared this strategic hardware complete on May 18 after a decade-long effort, with delivery next to the Neutral Beam Test Facility in Padova. That matters because heating systems are among the hardest engineering subsystems in large tokamaks, and a validated European beam source improves both ITER execution credibility and the industrial learning curve for future plants.

- [Kyoto Fusioneering Awarded Contract by QST to Develop Tritium Extraction and Accountancy System Prototypes for ITER Test Blanket System](https://kyotofusioneering.com/en/news/2026/05/15/3934) is a company-announced contract win for prototypes of the tritium extraction and accountancy systems for ITER’s test blanket program.
