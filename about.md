<p>The Fusion Conclusion is an independent publication by <a href="https://www.linkedin.com/in/dan-brunner/" rel="noreferrer">Dan Brunner</a> on fusion as a technical field, an industrial sector, and a capital allocation problem. It is written for investors, suppliers, operators, and serious observers who want a clear, technically grounded view of what is real, what is overstated, and what matters next.</p>
  
<p>I publish three kinds of work:</p>
<ol>
  <li>Fusion Fortnightly: separating the signal from the noise across the industry</li>
  <li>Technical-commercial deep dives: on companies, reactor concepts, and supply-chain bottlenecks</li>
  <li>Essays and market notes: on where fusion capital, engineering effort, and industrial demand are actually heading</li>
</ol>

<h2>About Dan</h2>

<p>I co-founded Commonwealth Fusion Systems and served as CTO, where I led technology strategy, intellectual property, and the early conceptual design of SPARC. I hold a PhD in Applied Plasma Physics from MIT. <a href="https://futuretech.partners/" rel="noreferrer">Through Future Tech Partners</a>, I advise investors and industrial companies on fusion and fusion-enabling technologies.</p>

<p>This publication is grounded in public information, first-principles reasoning, and my own analysis.</p>
  
<h2>AI policy</h2>

<p>While I use today's AI tools to supercharge my research, copy-edit my writing, and debug my coding, I commit to publishing only my original ideas and writings, not AI slop. Any slop here is due to good old-fashioned human error.</p>
